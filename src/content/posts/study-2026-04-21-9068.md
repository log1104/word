# Bible Study: Revelation 4

**You:** Study Revelation 4:4... Who are the 24 elders?

In **Revelation 4:4**, the apostle John is granted a vision of the heavenly throne room. Among the awe-inspiring sights—the lightning, the sea of glass, and the four living creatures—are twenty-four elders seated on twenty-four thrones. 

Identifying these figures requires looking closely at the details provided in the text and connecting them to the broader tapestry of Scripture.

### 1. The Meaning of "Elders"
The word used for elders is *presbyterous* ([Strong's G4245](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/4245.htm)). In the New Testament, this term consistently refers to leaders of the people of God or leaders within the church. 

While some suggest these could be a special class of angelic beings, angels are never called "elders" in Scripture. Furthermore, the descriptions of their attire and rewards strongly suggest they represent redeemed humanity rather than celestial beings.

### 2. The Symbols of Redemption and Victory
John describes three specific characteristics of these elders that point toward their identity as representatives of the redeemed:

*   **White Robes:** They are "clothed in white robes" (*leukois* [Strong's G3022](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/3022.htm)). Throughout Revelation, white robes are the promised attire for those who overcome and are purified by the blood of the Lamb. See [Revelation 7:9](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Revelation+7%3A9) and [Revelation 3:5](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Revelation+3%3A5).
*   **Golden Crowns:** They wear "golden crowns" (*stephanous* [Strong's G4735](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/4735.htm)). This specific Greek word refers to a victor’s crown or a wreath given to one who has finished a race or won a battle. This is the "crown of righteousness" promised to believers in [2 Timothy 4:8](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=2+Timothy+4%3A8) and the "crown of life" in [James 1:12](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=James+1%3A12).
*   **Thrones:** They sit on "thrones" (*thronous* [Strong's G2362](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/2362.htm)). Jesus specifically promised His followers that they would sit on thrones and reign with Him. See [Matthew 19:28](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Matthew+19%3A28) and [Revelation 3:21](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Revelation+3%3A21).

### 3. Why Twenty-Four?
The number twenty-four is highly significant in biblical structure. It most likely represents the totality of God’s people across both covenants:

*   **The 12 Tribes of Israel:** Representing the faithful under the Old Covenant.
*   **The 12 Apostles:** Representing the Church under the New Covenant.

We see this "12 + 12" pattern again in the description of the New Jerusalem in [Revelation 21:12-14](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Revelation+21%3A12-14), where the names of the twelve tribes are on the gates and the names of the twelve apostles are on the foundations. 

Additionally, in the Old Testament, King David divided the priesthood into **twenty-four divisions** to represent the whole body of priests in worship before God ([1 Chronicles 24:1-19](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=1+Chronicles+24%3A1-19)). The twenty-four elders act as a "royal priesthood" ([1 Peter 2:9](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=1+Peter+2%3A9)), representing all believers in the heavenly sanctuary.

### 4. Their Function: Constant Worship
The most important thing to notice about the twenty-four elders is not just who they *are*, but what they *do*. In [Revelation 4:10-11](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Revelation+4%3A10-11), they fall down before the One on the throne and cast their crowns before Him.

This act acknowledges that their victory, their righteousness, and their positions of authority are entirely derived from God. They serve as a model for us: even in the highest positions of heavenly honor, the focus remains entirely on the worthiness of the Lord.

### Summary
The twenty-four elders are best understood as **representatives of the entire redeemed people of God** (both Israel and the Church). They sit in positions of authority, clothed in the righteousness of Christ and crowned with the rewards of faithfulness, yet their primary occupation is the ceaseless worship of the Creator.

**You:** Study 1 John 1:1... why did John use Word of Life, is this unique expression and compare this with bread of life.

In the opening of his first epistle, the Apostle John provides a powerful testimony to the physical reality of Jesus Christ. By using the title "Word of life," John bridges the gap between the eternal nature of God and the tangible experience of the disciples.

### Why John Uses "Word of Life"

John chooses the expression "Word of life" to emphasize that Jesus is both the ultimate revelation of God and the source of all existence. 

1.  **The Word (Logos):** By using [Strong's G3056](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/3056.htm) (_Logos_), John connects this letter to the opening of his Gospel ([John 1:1](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=John+1%3A1)). In Greek thought, the _Logos_ was the underlying reason for the universe; in Jewish thought, it was the creative power of God. John identifies this "Word" as a person who was "from the beginning" ([Strong's G746](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/746.htm)).
2.  **The Life (Zōē):** The word for "life" ([Strong's G2222](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/2222.htm)) refers not just to biological existence, but to the uncreated, eternal life of God. John’s purpose is to show that this eternal life became "audible, visible, and touchable" in the person of Jesus.

John uses a string of sensory verbs—heard ([Strong's G191](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/191.htm)), seen ([Strong's G3708](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/3708.htm)), and touched ([Strong's G5584](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/5584.htm))—to combat early false teachings (like Gnosticism) which suggested that Jesus was merely a spirit and didn't have a real physical body. To John, the "Word of life" is not a philosophy; He is a person he has physically embraced.

### Is This a Unique Expression?

The specific phrase "the Word of life" is quite rare in the New Testament, making it a distinctively Johannine concept, though it does appear once in the writings of Paul.

*   [**Philippians 2:16**](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Philippians+2%3A16)**:** Paul encourages believers to "hold firmly to the word of life." In Paul's context, this refers to the Gospel message—the spoken and written word that brings salvation.
*   [**1 John 1:1**](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=1+John+1%3A1)**:** John’s usage is unique because he personifies the term. He isn't just talking about a message _about_ life; he is talking about the Person who _is_ Life. 

Interestingly, your Word Data shows that the phrase "this is" ([Word 19]) is actually the Greek preposition _peri_ ([Strong's G4012](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/4012.htm)), which means "concerning" or "about." A literal reading would be: "concerning the Word of life." John is saying that everything he has seen and touched is _concerning_ this eternal reality.

### Comparison: "Word of Life" vs. "Bread of Life"

John is the only biblical author to use both these titles for Jesus. While they both point to Jesus as the source of eternal existence, they emphasize different aspects of His ministry.

| Feature | The Word of Life ([1 John 1:1](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=1+John+1%3A1)) | The Bread of Life ([John 6:35](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=John+6%3A35)) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Primary Theme** | **Revelation & Reality.** Jesus is the visible expression of the invisible God. | **Sustenance & Satisfaction.** Jesus is the essential "food" for the human soul. |
| **Focus** | Focuses on the **Incarnation**: that which was from the beginning has become tangible. | Focuses on **Sufficiency**: those who come to Him will never hunger again. |
| **Human Response** | We **testify** to what we have seen and heard ([1 John 1:2-3](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=1+John+1%3A2-3)). | We **believe** and "eat" (appropriate His sacrifice) ([John 6:48-51](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=John+6%3A48-51)). |
| **Linguistic Root** | *Logos* (The Message/Reason) | *Artos* (The Bread/Sustenance) |

In summary, John uses "Word of Life" to tell us that God has spoken to us by becoming one of us. While the "Bread of Life" tells us how Jesus _sustains_ us, the "Word of Life" tells us how God has _revealed_ Himself to us in a way that we can truly know, see, and touch.

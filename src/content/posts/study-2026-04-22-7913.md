**You:** Study Revelation 5:6... who is the seven spirits of God?

**AI:**In Revelation 5:6, the "seven spirits of God" are identified as the seven eyes of the Lamb ([Strong's G721](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/721.htm)). To understand who these "spirits" ([Strong's G4151](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/4151.htm)) are, we must look at how the number seven is used in biblical prophecy and how these spirits relate to the Godhead.

### 1. The Fullness of the Holy Spirit
In the book of Revelation, the number seven ([Strong's G2033](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/2033.htm)) frequently represents completeness, perfection, or totality. Most faithful interpreters identify the "seven spirits" as a symbolic representation of the **Holy Spirit** in His perfect fullness and diverse operation.

This connection is rooted in [Isaiah 11:2](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Isaiah+11%3A2), which describes the sevenfold nature of the Spirit of the LORD resting upon the Messiah:
1. The Spirit of the LORD
2. The Spirit of wisdom
3. The Spirit of understanding
4. The Spirit of counsel
5. The Spirit of might
6. The Spirit of knowledge
7. The Spirit of the fear of the LORD

### 2. Evidence from the Divine Greeting
The strongest evidence that the "seven spirits" refers to the Holy Spirit is found in the opening of the book. In [Revelation 1:4–5](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Revelation+1%3A4-5), John offers a greeting of grace and peace from:
*   "Him who is, and who was, and who is to come" (The Father)
*   "The seven spirits before His throne" (The Holy Spirit)
*   "Jesus Christ, the faithful witness" (The Son)

Because these spirits are placed between the Father and the Son in a formal divine greeting, it is highly unlikely they are created angels. Only a member of the Trinity could be the source of divine grace and peace alongside the Father and the Son.

### 3. The Eyes of the Lamb
In our focal verse, Revelation 5:6, the seven spirits are described as the "eyes" of the Lamb. This imagery conveys two vital truths:

*   **Omniscience:** The Lamb (Jesus Christ) sees all things. As the text says, they are "sent out into all the earth" ([Strong's G649](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/649.htm)). This mirrors [Zechariah 4:10](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Zechariah+4%3A10), where the "seven eyes of the LORD" range throughout the earth, signifying God's perfect knowledge and watchful care.
*   **The Unity of Christ and the Spirit:** The Spirit is the "Spirit of Christ" ([Romans 8:9](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Romans+8%3A9)). By having the seven spirits as His eyes, Christ demonstrates that He acts and sees through the Holy Spirit. This aligns with Jesus' promise in [John 15:26](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=John+15%3A26) that He would send the Spirit from the Father to the world.

### 4. The Seven Burning Lamps
The "seven spirits" are also mentioned in [Revelation 4:5](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Revelation+4%3A5) as "seven lamps of fire" burning before the throne. This links back to the tabernacle's golden lampstand ([Exodus 25:37](https://www.openbible.info/labs/cross-references/search?q=Exodus+25%3A37)), which provided the only light in the Holy Place. This suggests that the Holy Spirit is the one who provides perfect illumination and reveals the glory of God to the Church.

### Summary
The "seven spirits of God" are not seven distinct individuals, but the **one Holy Spirit** portrayed in His numerical perfection. He is "sent out" ([Strong's G649](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/649.htm)) by the Lamb to execute God's will, provide perfect sight (omniscience) to the Savior, and bring the presence of God to the entire earth.

# Understanding Purity of Heart

**You:** Study Matthew 5:8... Focusnon understanding pure in heart

**AI:** The sixth Beatitude in [Matthew 5:8](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Matthew/5/8) says: *"Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God."* 

To understand what it means to be "pure in heart," we must look beyond mere outward behavior and examine the original language and the biblical definition of the "heart" as the control center of a person.

### The Meaning of "Pure" (*Katharos*)
The Greek word for "pure" is *katharos* ([Strong's G2513](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/2513.htm)). In the context of the New Testament, this word carries two primary meanings:

1.  **Cleansed from Defilement:** Like a garment that has been washed or a wound that has been scrubbed. This refers to being made clean from the stain of sin.
2.  **Unalloyed or Unmixed:** This describes something that is pure because it is not diluted. For example, gold is "pure" when the dross has been removed, or milk is "pure" when it has not been watered down.

In the Beatitudes, Jesus is describing a **singleness of heart**. A pure heart is an undivided heart—one that is not "double-minded." [James 4:8](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/James/4/8) echoes this when it commands, *"Purify your hearts, you double-minded."* To be pure in heart is to have a focused, sincere devotion to God without the mixture of selfish motives or hypocrisy.

### The Meaning of "Heart" (*Kardia*)
In the Bible, the "heart" (*kardia*, [Strong's G2588](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/2588.htm)) is not just the seat of emotions. It refers to the entire inner person: the mind, the will, and the desires. 

The heart is the "wellspring of life" ([Proverbs 4:23](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Proverbs/4/23)). Therefore, purity of heart is not just about having "good feelings" or following religious rules; it is about the internal transformation of our choices and our character. This stands in contrast to the Pharisees, whom Jesus criticized for cleaning the outside of the cup while the inside remained "full of greed and self-indulgence" ([Matthew 23:25-26](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Matthew/23/25-26)).

### Biblical Background: Who May Stand?
Jesus’ listeners would have immediately associated "pure in heart" with the requirements for entering God's presence in the Temple. [Psalm 24:3-4](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Psalm/24/3-4) asks: 

> *"Who may ascend the hill of the LORD? Who may stand in His holy place? He who has clean hands and a pure heart..."*

Under the Old Covenant, ceremonial purity was required to enter the Temple. However, Jesus raises the bar to the level of the spirit. True access to God requires a purity that no human can achieve on their own. This is why David cried out in [Psalm 51:10](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Psalm/51/10), *"Create in me a clean heart, O God."* It is a gift of God’s grace, not an achievement of human effort.

### The Promise: "They Will See God"
The reward for this purity is the greatest promise in Scripture: seeing God. This has two fulfillments:

1.  **The Present Reality:** Those with a pure (undivided) heart begin to "see" God’s hand at work in their lives, in creation, and in His Word. They have a spiritual perception that the "double-minded" lack.
2.  **The Future Hope:** This refers to the "Beatific Vision"—the moment when believers see Christ face to face. [1 John 3:2-3](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/1%20John/3/2-3) links these concepts perfectly: we shall see Him as He is, and this hope motivates us to purify ourselves just as He is pure.

### Summary for Study
To be "pure in heart" means to have an **undivided loyalty** to God. It is a heart that has been cleansed by the blood of Christ and is now focused on one thing: the glory of God. It is the opposite of hypocrisy; it is being the same person in the dark that you are in the light. 

As you study this, consider [Hebrews 12:14](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Hebrews/12/14): *"Work at living in peace with everyone, and work at living a holy life, for those who are not holy will not see the Lord."* Purity is the prerequisite for intimacy with God.

In the Hebrew mindset, the "heart" ([Strong's G2588](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/2588.htm)) was the seat of the will and the mind. To be "pure" (*katharos*) often meant to be "unalloyed"—like gold that has no other metals mixed in.

### 1. Purity as "Unmixed" (Singleness)

The "heart" (*kardia*, [Strong's G2588](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/2588.htm)) in Scripture is not just the place where we feel "pure thoughts." It is the seat of the **will**, the **intellect**, and the **intentions**.

When Jesus speaks of purity here, He is targeting the **"why"** behind our "what." 
*   A person might do a "good" thing (like giving to the poor) but have an "impure" heart because they are doing it to be seen by others ([Matthew 6:1-2](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Matthew/6/1-2)).
*   A "pure" heart is transparent. There is no hidden agenda, no "mask," and no "fine print." It is what King David called an "undivided heart" ([Psalm 86:11](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Psalm/86/11)).

The reason the pure in heart "will see" (*opsontai*, [Strong's G3708](https://biblehub.com/strongs/greek/3708.htm)) God is because their "spiritual eyes" are not clouded by competing loyalties. 

Think of a window:
*   If a window is covered in mud (obvious sin), you can’t see through it.
*   But even if the window is "clean" but made of distorted, wavy glass (mixed motives and hypocrisy), the view is still blurred.
*   A **pure** window is so clear you don't even see the glass—you only see what is on the other side. 

The "pure in heart" are those who have allowed God to "purify" their motives so that they no longer get in the way of seeing Him. As [1 John 3:2-3](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/1%20John/3/2-3) explains, the very act of looking forward to "seeing Him" is what drives us to "purify" ourselves now.

### Summary: It’s about Integrity
To be pure in heart is to have **integrity of soul**. It means you are the same person in the dark that you are in the light. It means your "yes" is "yes" ([Matthew 5:37](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Matthew/5/37)). It is a heart that has been purged of the "dross" of self-interest so that only a sincere desire for God remains. 

As [Psalm 24:3-4](https://bible-xref.247base.uk/Psalm/24/3-4) makes clear, this is the only way to "ascend the hill of the Lord"—not by being perfect, but by being **honest and undivided** before Him.
